Things I have built outside work, mostly because I wanted them to exist.

Scout

Scout reads the back of the packet so you do not have to. Indian grocery shelves, the same quick-commerce shelves I work on by day, are full of products that sound healthy and are not, and the truth is usually in small print on the back. You search for a packaged food, and Scout reads its ingredients and nutrition label and gives you a plain verdict: worth buying, or worth skipping, and if you should skip it, what to reach for instead. It is an attempt to put a nutritionist's skepticism behind a search box.

Nourish

Nourish is a small, private nutrition tracker: calories, protein, water, and seven-day averages, and very little else. There is no account to make and no cloud to trust; everything lives on your own device, and you can export it whenever you like. I built it because most tracking apps ask far more of you than they give back.
