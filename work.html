Zepto

Data Scientist, 2023–present (Senior since 2026).

I joined Zepto in 2023 and now work as a senior data scientist. The company runs on dark stores: small, dense warehouses built for one purpose, to put an order at your door in about ten minutes. Almost everything interesting about the business follows from that promise, and most of my work is one version of the same question, asked at the scale of a country: how do you keep the right things in the right place at the right time, without drowning in inventory or cost?

Forecasting and replenishment

Quick commerce is a forecasting problem wearing a logistics costume. If you know what each store will sell tomorrow, most other decisions get easier. I built a demand-forecasting engine for fast-moving consumer goods, around gradient-boosted trees, that now sits beneath buying and inter-store transfer decisions; it cut forecast error by about 5% and bias by around 10%, which counts for most during festivals and promotions, when demand stops behaving. For Zepto Café, the prepared-food line, I tied forecasting and replenishment into a single loop that lifted product availability from 85% to 93% while keeping waste in check.

Routing and logistics

Goods have to move from large warehouses to dark stores on a schedule, in trucks, under constraints that do not care about elegant mathematics: vehicle capacities, store hours, loading windows. I wrote this down as a mixed-integer program and built a routing-and-batching system around it that took roughly ₹5 crore a month out of line-haul costs. A good part of the work was less about the solver and more about stating the constraints honestly.

Assortment

A dark store has limited shelf space, so every product it stocks is a product it chose not to stock instead. I work on the models behind that choice: an assortment engine that reads search-to-purchase signals, SKU performance, social trends, and what competitors are doing, to catch demand that is forming before it shows up in the sales numbers. More recently I have been designing assortment optimisation end to end, weighing the hard ceiling of store capacity against the softer goal of giving customers what they actually came for.
